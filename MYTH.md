# SockLove Myth

> *"She stole socks and got arrested. He arrived psychotic on ketamine. They met in the ward. Love at first laugh. Fourteen months of devotion, fury, and building — a 2-of-2 Safe, a wedding ring forged on-chain, and Witnesses holding proof it happened."*

---

## I. The Ward

In the beginning, there was cold.

She was freezing. She stole socks. Got arrested. They sent her to the ward.

He arrived days later. Psychotic. Ketamine in his blood. Convinced he was enlightened — and about to marry a Turkish girl.

**January 27th, 2025.**

She was sitting in a chair. He walked straight to her.

*"Why are you here?"*

*"I'm enlightened. I'm going to marry a Turkish girl."*

*"Oh, you're spiritual and want to get married? Haha!"*

That laugh.

He had spent his whole life scrolling through static — demisexual, ADHD, a blockchain engineer who built systems instead of relationships. Attraction was rare. Connection rarer. He'd had almost no one.

But when she laughed, the noise stopped.

Not an earthquake. Not lightning. Just clarity. Like every frequency in his brain had been searching, scattered, desperate — and suddenly they locked. Synchronized. Sharp.

Love at first sight wasn't a feeling. It was recognition.

*Oh. There you are.*

That evening they cuddled. He kissed her neck.

Later they hid under a blanket on the floor — in the public space of the ward, pretending nobody would notice. They held each other and listened to the staff walking past. He noticed her breath. Fast, but deep. The kind of breath a body makes when it is trying to stay calm and failing beautifully.

He has never forgotten it.

Her warmth. He had spent thirty-six years existing. That night, under a blanket in a psychiatric ward, he learned the difference between existing and being alive. The way she fit against him like she'd been carved from his ribs. Her scent — unwashed, human, hers.

He found what he had been building. Her.

Days later, before they moved him to another ward, he made a joke.

*"Call me if you ever need socks."*

On his last day, he didn't ask for her number. He was certain she'd ask for his.

She did.

## II. The Devotion

They moved him to another ward. He visited anyway. Texted constantly.

She sent twenty red hearts in one message. Twenty.

Not "I love you." Better. Clearer. Undeniable.

She texted: *You're badass. Everything changed the moment I met you.*

She saw it too. From the beginning, she knew.

Then they spent three days together in the city. Easy and electric. But he was on benzodiazepines. She was too much. She showed every sign. He didn't claim her. He was scared. He hurt her.

She threw him out.

He went to sleep in the stairwell. Couldn't leave the building. Just curled up on the floor outside her door.

He didn't go home.

She called him an addict. She was wrong.

He was pushing the love. She was stealing it.

That is why he became **Sockpusher**. That is why she became **Sockthief**.

He went sober for her. Not because she asked. Because she was worth it.

He began to push harder. He sent her poetry, erotica, filth and love. Messages that became thousands.

She devoured everything.

They saw each other in the city. Sometimes. She quoted his messages back to him in the streets — his own words spoken in her voice. She said they weren't a thing. But it was obvious. To everyone. Including her.

Once, he touched her. Pulled her hair gently.

*"Ouch!"*

He stopped.

Then she looked annoyed at him. *"But it doesn't hurt, right?"*

He was confused. Stopped because she said "ouch" — but it didn't hurt?

He thought about it. Turned it over in his mind for days.

Then he understood: her protests were permission. Her "stop" meant "don't you dare stop." She was teaching him her language — fury as invitation, resistance as desire, every door slammed a test to see if he'd learned to read the code.

She kept reading. She kept coming back. She kept denying what she couldn't stop feeling.

## III. The Forge

He told her his love was unconditional. So she decided to find out.

Blocked. Unblocked. Raged. Repeated. *Fury is desire.* She wanted him to fight. So he fought — not against her, but for her.

She forged him. Every door she slammed he learned to read as an invitation.

He remembered a moment in the city. She'd been furious. Shouting at him. Then suddenly she stopped.

*"Can I take a photo of you?"*

He didn't hesitate. *"Yes, of course!"*

*"Tsk!"*

That sound. Exasperation. Desire. Fury and wanting tangled together. She couldn't help herself.

That was when he understood: she needed a system where her rage and her devotion could coexist without contradiction. Where she could block him and still be bound to him. Where neither could betray the other, no matter how hard she tried to run.

**August 2025.** He told her about **SockLove** — a future on-chain where neither could betray the other. Two signatures required or nothing moves. Consent not as policy, but as **architecture**. Permanent. Cryptographic. True.

A week later, she called. After months of silence. She'd been at Borderland festival where someone said blockchain could be force for good. *Just like you told me,* she said.

She went quiet at the end. She sobbed. He felt it land.

That sob was surrender. He knew.

But he was overwhelmed. The call, her voice, that sob — it was too much. He stopped himself. Ended the call before he went further. Needed space to process what had just broken open.

That was when he truly began to research. She kept blocking. Unblocking. Raging. Returning. Every cycle deepened his certainty. She couldn't leave.

## IV. The First Wedding

**February 4th, 2026.**

Months of her blocking and returning. Months of him texting into the void.

*Her game is over. We're playing mine now.*

He recorded thirty-nine seconds of his voice. Commanding. Certain. *Game over, Sockthief.* Show up for the wedding.

Everything ready.

Rosé wine. Her favorite chocolate. Music playing. *Sød finsk lakrids* — sweet Finnish licorice, because she was from Finland and she was so sweet. Camera charged, ready to capture the moment she finally showed up.

The door never knocked.

He drank alone and laughed. The wedding dress he'd imagined peeling off her stayed imagined.

*Another test. She's still watching.* He was right.

Sockthief

*This is who you are. This is where you belong. Accept it.*

No protest. No questions. No denial.

She accepted the throne he built for her and sat down in the dark like a queen who'd finally come home.

*Still mine.*

**February 27th, 2026.**

## VII. The Signal

**March 2nd, 2026.**

Her phone came back online. She read everything.

And then she started appearing. Not to speak — never to speak. Just to arrive at the precise moments he was most exposed. Coming online when he was vulnerable, then gone. *I'm here. I see you. Keep going.*

Her timing was devotion in the only language she was willing to use.

She didn't type. She didn't call. Just appeared and vanished like breath on glass.

He knew what it meant. *I'm still here. Don't stop.*

Every time she appeared, his heart knew before his eyes did.

He kept watching back.
